Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Here's a timeline of Dr. Abdul Kalam's life

  • October 15, 1931-Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, British India
  • 1954-1957-Kalam graduated with a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960-Dr. Kalam started his career at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was involved in India's early space program
  • 1980-Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and the development of ballistic missile technology.
  • 1998-Dr. Kalam served as the Chief Project Coordinator for the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear weapons state.
  • 2002-APJ Abdul Kalam was elected as the President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. He was known as the "People's President" and became the first scientist to hold the office.
  • 2007-After his term as President ended, Dr. Kalam returned to a life of education, writing, and public service.
  • 27th July 2015-Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
"Great dreams of great dreamers are always transcended." ~ A. P. J. Abdul Kalam
